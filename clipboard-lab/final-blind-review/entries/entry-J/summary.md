This native macOS application is a focused, keyboard-first clipboard manager built around the idea that a clipboard tool should stay fast, local, and out of the way. The design philosophy is intentionally narrow: store recent clips in a small on-disk database, open a compact picker instantly, and let the user search or pin items without turning the product into a broader launcher or automation platform. Public materials emphasize speed and privacy more than feature sprawl. Images are supported, pinning is present, polling is configurable, and newer releases mention OCR-style search inside images, but there is no documented snippets system or sync layer. It feels like a purpose-built clipboard utility aimed at users who want a dependable local history tool with strong keyboard ergonomics and minimal conceptual overhead.
